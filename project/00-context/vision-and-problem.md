# Vision and Problem

## Vision

`personal-crm` is a personal relationship management application that helps one user centralize, understand, and maintain important human relationships over time.

The product should become a trusted memory layer for contacts, interactions, reminders, and relationship context. It is designed for personal use first, while keeping future expansion options visible without forcing that complexity into the MVP.

## Target User

- Primary user: a single individual managing friends, family, and important personal or professional contacts.
- Current account model: one account maps to one user.
- Future possibility: broader collaboration or richer tenancy may be explored later, but it is not part of the approved implementation baseline.

## Core Problem

Important contacts are fragmented across services such as Google Contacts, social networks, messaging tools, and calendars. Because relationship context is scattered, the user:

- forgets who they last saw or messaged,
- loses track of important follow-ups,
- cannot easily see relationship health over time,
- and has no unified place to capture notes, interactions, and reminders.

## Desired Outcome

The application should make it easy to:

- import contacts into one place,
- organize them with meaningful classification,
- record interactions over time,
- create reminders for follow-up,
- and later introduce smarter guidance for staying in touch.

## Product Principles

- Privacy first: contact and relationship data are sensitive.
- Scope discipline: keep MVP narrow and usable.
- Extensible foundation: leave room for richer integrations later.
- Fast retrieval: the product must act like an external memory, not a slow CRM.
- Human-centered value: reminders and suggestions must support real relationships, not generic productivity theater.
